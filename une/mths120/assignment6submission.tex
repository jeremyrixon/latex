\begin{align}
f(x) - g(x) &= x^3 - x \\
               &= x(x-1)(x+1)
\end{align} 
So \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) intersect at \(x = 0\), \(x = -1\) and \(x = 1\).
\begin{align}
\int  x^3 - x \, dx &= \frac{x^4}{4} - \frac{x^2}{2} + C\\
\int_{-1}^{0} x^3 - x \, dx &=  0 - (\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{2}) = \frac{1}{4} \\
\int_{0}^{1} x^3 - x \, dx &= (\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{2}) - 0 = -\frac{1}{4} \\
Area &= \frac{1}{2}
\end{align}
